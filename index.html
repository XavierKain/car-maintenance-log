Perspective

The cost of neglect

A skipped oil change is a $75 savings today and a $4,000 engine repair next year. A forgotten timing belt is a tow truck and a week without your car. These aren't scare tactics. They're just math.

Most people don't neglect their vehicles on purpose. They simply lose track. They can't remember if the last tire rotation was in March or September. They're not sure if the brake fluid has ever been changed. Small uncertainties compound into expensive surprises.

A simple log changes everything. When you can see your full maintenance history at a glance, you make better decisions. You catch the small things before they become big things. You keep your vehicles running longer, safer, and cheaper.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is this a subscription or a one-time purchase?
One-time purchase. You pay $4.99 once and the app is yours forever. There are no recurring charges, no premium tiers, and no hidden fees.
Does it work offline?
Yes. Your data is stored locally on your device. You can add service records, view your history, and manage vehicles without an internet connection. Everything syncs when you're back online.
How many vehicles can I track?
There's no limit. Add as many cars, trucks, motorcycles, or any other vehicles as you need. Each gets its own separate maintenance timeline and reminder schedule.
Can I set reminders by mileage and by date?
Yes. You can set reminders based on a calendar date, a mileage interval, or both. For example, you might set an oil change reminder for every 5,000 miles or every 6 months, whichever comes first.
Can I attach receipts and photos?
Absolutely. Each service entry supports photo attachments. Snap a picture of your receipt, invoice, or inspection report and it's linked directly to that record for easy reference.
Is my data private?
Your maintenance data is stored locally on your device. We don't collect, sell, or share your personal information. Your vehicle history is yours and yours alone.
Is the app open source?
Yes. The source code is available on GitHub. You can inspect the code, report issues, or contribute.

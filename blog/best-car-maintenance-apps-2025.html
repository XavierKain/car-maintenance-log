Your car needs regular maintenance to stay safe, reliable, and efficient. But keeping track of what was done, when it's due next, and how much it all costs is surprisingly hard. A notebook gets lost. A spreadsheet is clunky on your phone. And your memory is terrible at remembering whether you changed the oil 4,000 or 7,000 miles ago.

That's where car maintenance apps come in. They give you a dedicated place to log services, set reminders, track costs, and keep a digital record that follows your vehicle through its entire life. In 2025, there are more options than ever — but they vary wildly in features, pricing, and privacy practices.

We tested seven of the most popular car maintenance apps to help you find the right one. We evaluated each on features, ease of use, pricing, offline capability, and data privacy. Here's what we found.

Why Use a Maintenance App?

Before diving into the comparison, let's address why you'd want a dedicated app in the first place. After all, you could just use your phone's notes app or a calendar.

The answer is structure and automation. A dedicated maintenance app gives you:

  • Organized history: Every service logged with date, mileage, cost, and notes — searchable and sortable
  • Smart reminders: Notifications based on date or mileage, not just calendar dates
  • Cost analysis: Automatic calculations of total spending by category, vehicle, and time period
  • Receipt storage: Photo attachments that replace fading paper receipts
  • Multi-vehicle support: Separate profiles for each vehicle in your household
  • Export capability: Data you can take with you, share with buyers, or use for taxes

A notes app can't do any of this without significant manual effort. A dedicated app turns a tedious chore into a 30-second task at the mechanic's counter.

What We Looked For

We evaluated each app on these criteria:

  • Core features: Service logging, reminders, cost tracking, photo receipts, multi-vehicle support
  • Ease of use: How quickly can you log a service? Is the interface intuitive?
  • Pricing: Free tier vs. paid. Subscription vs. one-time purchase. Value for money.
  • Offline capability: Does it work without internet? Critical for logging at the shop.
  • Privacy: Where is your data stored? Is it sold to third parties? Do you need an account?
  • Export: Can you get your data out in a useful format?
  • Platform: Available on iOS, Android, and/or web?

1. Car Maintenance Log

Price: $4.99 one-time
Platforms: Web (PWA), iOS, Android
Best for: Privacy-conscious users who want a simple, complete tool with no subscriptions

Car Maintenance Log takes a refreshingly different approach to vehicle tracking. Instead of requiring an account and syncing to the cloud, it stores everything locally on your device using localStorage. This means it works completely offline, starts instantly, and your data never leaves your phone.

The interface is clean and focused. Adding a service takes about 30 seconds: tap the vehicle, tap "Add Service," fill in the details, optionally snap a receipt photo, and you're done. The app supports unlimited vehicles, each with its own maintenance timeline, cost breakdown, and reminder schedule.

Reminders can be set by date or mileage — a feature that sounds basic but is missing from several competitors. The cost tracking view shows spending by category and month with clear charts. Data export is available in CSV and PDF formats, making it easy to share maintenance history when selling a vehicle.

The pricing model is its strongest selling point: $4.99 once, forever. No subscription, no premium tier, no ads. Every feature is included. In an era where everything wants $5-10 per month, this is a breath of fresh air.

Pros:

  • Completely offline — works without internet
  • Data stored locally — maximum privacy
  • One-time $4.99 payment — no subscription
  • Unlimited vehicles with individual tracking
  • Photo receipts included (not paywalled)
  • Clean, fast interface
  • CSV and PDF export

Cons:

  • No cloud sync between devices (trade-off for privacy)
  • No OBD-II integration
  • No community features (fuel price sharing, etc.)

2. Drivvo

Price: Free with ads / $5.99/year for Pro
Platforms: iOS, Android
Best for: Users who want detailed fuel tracking alongside maintenance logs

Drivvo has been around for years and has built a solid reputation for comprehensive vehicle management. The free version covers basic maintenance logging and fuel tracking with ads. The Pro version ($5.99/year) removes ads, adds photo receipts, cloud backup, and multi-vehicle support.

Drivvo's strength is its fuel tracking depth. It calculates MPG, cost per mile, fuel cost trends, and even compares fuel economy between fill-ups. For people who want granular fuel data alongside maintenance tracking, Drivvo is excellent.

The interface is functional but slightly dated compared to newer apps. Navigation involves several screens to log a simple service. The learning curve is mild but present — new users might spend a few minutes figuring out where everything is.

Offline support is partial. You can view existing data offline, but some features require connectivity. Cloud sync is available for Pro users, which is convenient for multi-device access but means your data is stored on Drivvo's servers.

Pros:

  • Excellent fuel tracking and analytics
  • Income tracking for rideshare/business use
  • Cloud backup and sync (Pro)
  • Long track record — established and reliable

Cons:

  • Subscription required for key features (photo receipts, multi-vehicle)
  • Interface feels slightly dated
  • Partial offline support
  • Free version has ads

3. Fuelly

Price: Free
Platforms: Web, iOS, Android
Best for: Fuel economy enthusiasts and community-oriented users

Fuelly is primarily a fuel tracking app with a community angle. Users can compare their fuel economy with other owners of the same vehicle, view average MPG data by make and model, and participate in fuel economy challenges.

The maintenance tracking feature exists but is minimal. You can log services, but there are no reminders, no photo receipts, and no cost analytics. If maintenance tracking is your priority, Fuelly falls short.

Where Fuelly excels is its community data. With millions of fill-ups logged, it provides remarkably accurate average MPG data for virtually every vehicle. If you want to know whether your 2019 Civic is getting better or worse fuel economy than average, Fuelly tells you instantly.

The app requires an internet connection and a user account. All data is stored in the cloud. The interface is clean and the web version works well, making it accessible across devices.

Pros:

  • Completely free
  • Excellent community MPG data
  • Cross-platform with web access
  • Clean, simple interface

Cons:

  • Minimal maintenance tracking features
  • No service reminders
  • No photo receipts
  • Requires internet connection
  • Account required

4. CARFAX Car Care

Price: Free
Platforms: iOS, Android
Best for: Users who want maintenance recommendations based on their specific vehicle

CARFAX Car Care leverages CARFAX's massive vehicle database to provide personalized maintenance schedules. Enter your VIN or license plate, and the app pulls your vehicle's service history (from participating shops) and creates a recommended maintenance schedule based on your car's make, model, year, and mileage.

This automatic schedule generation is the app's killer feature. Instead of looking up your car's recommended service intervals, CARFAX tells you what's due and when. It even pulls in recall information and service bulletins from the manufacturer.

The downside is that CARFAX Car Care is more of a recommendation engine than a comprehensive logging tool. You can manually log services, but the cost tracking is basic and there are no photo receipt attachments. The app also requires an internet connection and stores your data on CARFAX's servers.

Privacy is worth considering here. CARFAX is in the business of vehicle data. While the app is free, your vehicle and service data contributes to CARFAX's database, which is then sold to dealerships, insurance companies, and car buyers. If you're the type who prefers data privacy, this is a significant trade-off.

Pros:

  • Free
  • Automatic maintenance schedules based on your specific vehicle
  • Recall and service bulletin notifications
  • Pulls existing service history from CARFAX database

Cons:

  • Basic manual logging features
  • No photo receipts
  • No cost analytics
  • Data stored on and used by CARFAX
  • Requires internet

5. aCar

Price: Free / $4.99 one-time for Pro
Platforms: Android only
Best for: Android users who want a mature, full-featured vehicle manager

aCar (short for "a Car manager") is a veteran Android app that's been refined over many years. The Pro version ($4.99) is comprehensive: fuel tracking, maintenance logging, expense categories, statistics, reminders, and photo attachments.

The app supports local storage with optional cloud backup through Google Drive. This hybrid approach gives you offline access while protecting against data loss. Statistics are detailed — you can view costs by category, period, and vehicle with various chart types.

The interface shows its age. aCar follows Android design patterns from several years ago, which means it's functional but not beautiful. For users who prioritize features over aesthetics, this won't matter. For those who want a modern, polished experience, it might feel dated.

The biggest limitation is platform availability. aCar is Android-only — there's no iOS version and no web app. If you switch to iPhone or want to access your data from a computer, you're stuck.

Pros:

  • Mature, full-featured app
  • $4.99 one-time purchase (no subscription)
  • Local storage with optional Google Drive backup
  • Detailed statistics and charts
  • Works offline

Cons:

  • Android only — no iOS or web
  • Dated interface
  • Steeper learning curve
  • Photo receipts only in Pro version

6. Simply Auto

Price: Free / $5.99/year for Premium
Platforms: iOS, Android
Best for: Users who want a modern interface with GPS-based trip tracking

Simply Auto is a newer entrant with a modern, well-designed interface. The free version covers basic service logging for one vehicle. Premium ($5.99/year) unlocks multiple vehicles, photo receipts, cloud sync, GPS trip tracking, and advanced statistics.

The standout feature is GPS trip tracking, which automatically logs your trips and calculates mileage. This is particularly useful for business users who need to track vehicle use for tax purposes. The trip log integrates with the maintenance schedule, so reminders are based on actual driving distance.

The interface is the most polished of any app in this comparison. Adding entries is quick and intuitive, statistics are presented in clean charts, and the overall experience feels premium.

The subscription model is the main drawback. $5.99/year isn't expensive, but it adds up over the life of vehicle ownership. Key features like multi-vehicle support and photo receipts being locked behind the paywall means the free version is essentially a trial.

Pros:

  • Modern, polished interface
  • GPS trip tracking
  • Cloud sync across devices
  • Business-friendly trip logging

Cons:

  • Subscription for key features
  • Free version limited to one vehicle
  • Partial offline support only
  • Newer app — less established track record

7. Jerry

Price: Free
Platforms: iOS, Android
Best for: Users looking for insurance savings alongside basic maintenance tracking

Jerry is primarily an insurance comparison app that added vehicle maintenance tracking as a secondary feature. The app compares insurance quotes from multiple providers and can help you switch to a cheaper policy automatically.

The maintenance tracking is basic: you can log services and set simple reminders. There are no photo receipts, minimal cost analytics, and no export functionality. It feels like an afterthought compared to the insurance features.

The biggest concern with Jerry is privacy. The app collects extensive data about your driving habits, vehicle, and personal information. This data is shared with insurance partners and used for targeted recommendations. If you value privacy, Jerry is the worst option on this list.

That said, if you're primarily looking to save on car insurance and want basic maintenance tracking as a bonus, Jerry can be useful. Many users report saving $500-800 per year on insurance through the app. Just go in with eyes open about what data you're sharing.

Pros:

  • Free
  • Insurance comparison can save significant money
  • Simple interface

Cons:

  • Maintenance tracking is minimal — clearly a secondary feature
  • Extensive data collection and sharing with partners
  • No photo receipts, no export, no cost analytics
  • Requires internet connection
  • Aggressive push notifications

The Verdict

There's no single "best" app for everyone. It depends on what you prioritize:

  • Best overall: Car Maintenance Log — Complete features, one-time price, full offline, maximum privacy. If you want a no-nonsense tool that does everything without a subscription or data harvesting, this is it.
  • Best for fuel tracking: Drivvo — The deepest fuel analytics and cost-per-mile calculations.
  • Best for community data: Fuelly — Compare your fuel economy with millions of other drivers.
  • Best for automatic schedules: CARFAX Car Care — VIN-based maintenance recommendations with recall alerts.
  • Best for Android power users: aCar — Mature, feature-rich, one-time purchase.
  • Best interface: Simply Auto — The most polished design with GPS trip tracking.
  • Best for insurance savings: Jerry — Maintenance tracking is basic, but insurance comparison can save real money.

Our recommendation for most people is to prioritize three things: offline capability (because you need to log services at the shop, not just at home), privacy (your vehicle data shouldn't be monetized), and fair pricing (one-time purchases respect your wallet more than subscriptions).

On those three criteria, Car Maintenance Log stands out. It works offline, stores data locally on your device, costs $4.99 once, and includes every feature — photo receipts, unlimited vehicles, reminders, cost tracking, and export. It's the tool we built because we couldn't find one that got all three right.
